About Me

10+ years in software development. My first taste in programming was over 20 years ago in elementary school when I picked up an Algebra book and it contained some small BASIC code snippets. I plugged them into my computer and they worked! I continuously filled my curiousity and learned more and more about computers and software. I was soon considered the computer expert on our block.

That title followed me into college where I received my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with minors in Computer Science and English. In my senior year, our team won an award for our educational math game written in flash.

After graduating, I turned down a few technical support positions because I knew I wanted to write code. Luckily, I was soon hired at UniFocus working on paper and online surveys. For the paper surveys, I used OCR technogly and C++ to import the data and SQL Server to query the results. For the online surveys, I used HTML, CSS, and a touch of JS to generate the surveys. Throughout my time at UniFocus, I worked on various other projects including testing .Net projects, building dashboards with Flex, and eventually leading our small Business Intelligence team which used a combination of technologies such as SQL Server, Cognos, and BIRT.

After 8 years at UniFocus, I moved onto a new position at Petmate as an Applications Developer. At Petmate, I have worked on a large number of projects including Oracle reports, eCommerce, and various one-off projects. Some of the one-off projects are sweepstakes pages, a store locator service, and a service to retrieve profiles.

I am currently attending an intensive 6 month boot camp dedicated to designing and building full-stack web applications at SMU. Skills learned consist of HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, Firebase, Node Js, MySQL, MongoDB, Express, Nightmare.JS, and React Js.
